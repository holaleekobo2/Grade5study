Rules at the Swimming Pool
It is a hot summer day. Amy and her little brother Ben are at the community swimming pool with their mum. Before they enter, a lifeguard explains the rules. "Everyone has to wear a swimming cap," he says firmly. "Children can't run on the wet tiles because it is very slippery. You should walk slowly and carefully around the pool. Ben, you are small, so you have to stay in the shallow area at all times. You shouldn't splash water on other swimmers — it is not polite. If you feel tired, you should rest quietly on the bench." Amy listens attentively and nods. She is ready to enjoy a safe and fun afternoon.

A Letter from Camp
Dear Mum and Dad,
I am having a great time at nature camp! Our counsellor, Ms. Park, told us the rules on the first day. We have to wake up early every morning and make our beds neatly. We can't use our phones after 9 PM because it disturbs everyone's sleep. When we go on a night walk, we have to stay together as a group. We shouldn't wander off alone — it can be dangerous in the dark. Ms. Park also said we should treat the forest respectfully. That means we can't pick flowers or drop litter. Last night, we saw fireflies flash brightly in the trees. It was magical!
Love, Chloe
