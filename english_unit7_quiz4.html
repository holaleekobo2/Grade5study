P5 English Practice: Unit 7

Focus: Future Tense (Predictions & Questions) & Coordinating Conjunctions

Part A: Grammar & Usage (Future Predictions)
1. Scientists predict that the weather ______ warmer next year, ______ the ice will melt faster.
2. "______ we still have enough water in the future?" — "No, we ______."
3. Which sentence is grammatically correct?
4. There won't ______ any pollution if we ______ use gas.
Part B: Coordinating Conjunctions (and, or, but)
5. I want to save the planet, ______ I don't know how to start.
6. Should we turn off the lights ______ turn off the TV first?
7. In the future, the air will be clean ______ the water will be fresh.
8. We can travel by bike ______ by bus; both are better than cars.
Part C: Sentence Transformation & Error Identification
9. Change the statement to a question: "Computers will be much bigger in the future."
10. Find the error: "I (A) believe (B) that we (C) won't to have (D) cars one day."
11. Combine these sentences: "The glacier is melting. We must save energy."
12. Choose the correct negative response: "Will there be floods?"
Part D: Reading Logic & Comprehension
Save Energy, Save the Future!
In many countries, cars use gas, but this creates pollution. In the future, Luke and Mia believe we won’t use gas anymore. We will use energy from the sun or water instead. The air will be cleaner, but we must act now. If we don’t turn off the lights and recycle garbage, the glaciers will continue to melt and there will be floods. Will you help save the planet?
